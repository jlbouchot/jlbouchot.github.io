Who am I?

Passionate about mathematics and computer science, I work on making both worlds meet and get the best out of each other. I have had and still am having a dual academic and engineering career in which I let industrial questions drive my research interest and let my engineering problems benefit from current research. In parallel to research and industrial problem solving, I mentor students in their learning journeys.

Some key points

I am a fervent believer in Open science and reproducible research. As such most of my work and all of my recent works can be found online on various platforms: my research page; my list of publications; my software on GitHub; some recent papers on arXiv. You may also drop me an email, should you need more references. I have also contributed to some open source projects, such as

  • dynawo: a suite of simulation tools for power systems. In particular I work on automatically controling voltage on a (very) large high voltage network.
  • powsybl: PoWerSystemBlocks. In particular I have implemented a few functions regarding remedial actions and passed on some features to the python API: PyPowSyBl.
  • CSPDE: Compressed Sensing for PDEs. This is pretty much all in-house and is a tool to compute numerical solutions to parametric PDEs through generalized polynomial chaos.

I am very gereatful to have had the opportunity to teach and talk in various places around the world, including:

  • Southeast Asian Mathematical Society School on Modern Trends in Signal and Data Processing which took place in December 2022 in Manila.
  • High performance AI in the industry within the Teratec forum in June 2022. I talked about robust and stochastic combinatorial optimization on an HPC.
  • Gene Golub SIAM Summer School on the Theory and Practice of Deep Learning which took place (virtually online) in South Africa in July 2021.
  • Summer program on Mathematical Signal Processing and Data Analysis within the Academia Sinica in Taipei in Summer 2019.

As a researcher, I am generally interested in applications of sparse approximation and signal processing to general mathematical and engineering problems. I have contributed to many very diverse areas including (in non logical order) Optimal Coherence Tomography; Numerical solutions to parametric PDEs; Numerical recovery of sparse signals; Metagenomics; ...
